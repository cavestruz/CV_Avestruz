\resheading{Research Interests} 

During my PhD, I studied how baryonic processes in galaxy clusters,
such as feedback from active galactic nuclei and non-equilibrium
electrons, affect observations in the X-ray and microwave. I have
recently transitioned to developing the use of machine learning
algorithms in application to upcoming large survey datasets, such as
LSST and Euclid.  In particular, I am building an automated pipeline
to identify strong lensing systems.

{\bf Areas:} Galaxies, galaxy clusters, large-scale structure,
cosmological simulations, hydrodynamic simulations, high-energy
astrophysics, X-ray observations, Sunyaev-Zeldovich effect, strong
lensing, machine learning, galaxy formation \& evolution.

{\bf Technical Skills:} High Performance Computing, Parallel
Computing; Programming Languages: Python, C, OpenMP/MPI; Astrophysics
Software: developer for and user of Adaptive Refinement Tree code
